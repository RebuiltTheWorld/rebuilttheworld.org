# RTW Homepage Sections
## Rebuilt The World
### Version: v0.1

---

## Purpose of this document

This document breaks the RTW homepage into clearly defined content sections.

Its purpose is to describe:

- what each homepage section should contain,
- what each section is supposed to do,
- what tone it should carry,
- and what level of detail belongs there.

This is a section-level content specification document.

It sits between the homepage wireframe and the future coded implementation.

---

## Section order

The current recommended homepage section order is:

1. Hero
2. What RTW is
3. Why it exists
4. System layers
5. Mission extension
6. Entry paths
7. LAB and Archive
8. Signals
9. Closing call

This order should remain stable in the first version.

---

## Section 1 — Hero

### Purpose
Create immediate orientation, identity, and emotional direction.

### Required elements
- eyebrow line
- main headline
- hero statement
- supporting paragraph
- primary CTAs
- logo presence

### Content role
The hero should answer:
- What is this?
- Why should I care?
- Is this serious?

### Tone
- strong
- clear
- calm
- mission-driven

### Length guidance
- eyebrow: very short
- headline: very short
- hero statement: one sentence
- support text: 2 short paragraphs maximum

### CTA recommendation
- Explore the System
- Read the Manifesto
- Enter the LAB

### Notes
Do not overload this section with deep theory.
Its job is recognition and orientation.

---

## Section 2 — What RTW is

### Purpose
Provide a compact, stable explanation.

### Required elements
- heading
- one short main paragraph
- one optional supporting paragraph

### Content role
This section should explain that RTW is:
- more than a website,
- more than a brand,
- and more than a documentation project.

It should define RTW as a structured system.

### Tone
- plain
- confident
- non-hyped
- explanatory

### Length guidance
2 short paragraphs maximum.

### Notes
A visitor should leave this section with less ambiguity than they had at entry.

---

## Section 3 — Why it exists

### Purpose
Explain the need behind RTW.

### Required elements
- heading
- rationale paragraph
- emphasis on overlooked potential and self-directed growth

### Content role
This section should show that RTW exists because:
- many people with real potential are structurally overlooked,
- some builders grow outside conventional systems,
- and meaningful development often needs a different kind of environment.

### Tone
- human
- serious
- grounded
- respectful

### Length guidance
1–3 short paragraphs.

### Notes
This section gives the homepage moral and human depth.

---

## Section 4 — System layers

### Purpose
Show that RTW is layered and structured.

### Required elements
- heading
- short intro
- 4 layer blocks

### Required layer blocks
- Public Layer
- Living LAB
- Archive Layer
- Proof Layer

### Content role
This section should explain that RTW is not flat.
It contains:
- an orientation layer,
- a working layer,
- a preservation layer,
- and a trace/integrity layer.

### Tone
- structured
- precise
- readable
- calm

### Length guidance
- intro: 1 short paragraph
- each layer block: 1 short paragraph or 2 short lines

### Notes
This is one of the most important homepage sections.
It turns abstract depth into visible structure.

---

## Section 5 — Mission extension

### Purpose
Express the moral seriousness of RTW through its commitment to vulnerable children and young people.

### Required elements
- heading
- 1 core paragraph
- optional CTA to dedicated page

### Content role
This section should make visible that RTW cares about:
- protection,
- dignity,
- support,
- hidden potential,
- and those most easily left behind.

### Tone
- sincere
- restrained
- serious
- compassionate without sentimentality

### Length guidance
1–2 short paragraphs.

### Notes
This section must not feel performative.
It should feel real and integrated.

---

## Section 6 — Entry paths

### Purpose
Help different kinds of visitors see how they might enter RTW.

### Required elements
- heading
- short intro
- 4 entry cards or blocks

### Recommended entry paths
- For Builders
- For Thinkers
- For Future Readers
- For the Overlooked

### Content role
This section should translate philosophy into access paths.

It should tell visitors:
there is more than one legitimate way to enter this system.

### Tone
- open
- respectful
- clear
- invitational without being soft

### Length guidance
- intro: 1 short paragraph
- each path: title + 1 short paragraph

### Notes
This is where RTW begins to feel navigable.

---

## Section 7 — LAB and Archive

### Purpose
Show that RTW balances active building and preserved continuity.

### Required elements
- heading
- LAB block
- Archive block
- bridge text

### Content role
This section should communicate:
- the LAB is where development happens,
- the Archive is where continuity is preserved,
- both are essential.

### Tone
- balanced
- clear
- durable
- system-oriented

### Length guidance
- each block: 1 short paragraph
- bridge text: 1 short line or sentence

### Notes
This section helps distinguish RTW from both static documentation and chaotic experimentation.

---

## Section 8 — Signals

### Purpose
Introduce the idea of breadcrumb-like discovery.

### Required elements
- heading
- short explanatory paragraph
- optional future list of signal items

### Content role
This section should explain that signals are:
- fragments,
- notes,
- clues,
- and paths toward deeper layers.

### Tone
- intriguing
- structured
- not overly mysterious

### Length guidance
1 short paragraph plus optional list.

### Notes
Signals can remain lightweight in version one.

---

## Section 9 — Closing call

### Purpose
End with identity, momentum, and a clear next action.

### Required elements
- closing heading
- short paragraph
- final CTA row

### Content role
This section should reinforce:
- clarity,
- continuity,
- deliberate building,
- and invitation to continue deeper.

### Tone
- calm
- strong
- memorable
- durable

### Length guidance
1 short paragraph plus CTA set.

### Recommended CTAs
- Start with the About page
- Read the Manifesto
- Explore the Archive

### Notes
Do not make this section sound like a sales close.
It should feel like a final orientation point.

## Content constraints

Each homepage section should remain:

- short,
- readable,
- structurally clear,
- and distinct from the others.

Avoid:
- repeating the same claim in every section,
- inserting too much theory,
- or making every block equally dense.

Each section needs a clear job.
