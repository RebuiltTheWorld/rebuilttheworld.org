# RTW Homepage Wireframe
## Rebuilt The World
### Version: v0.1

---

## Purpose of this document

This document defines the first structural wireframe for the **Rebuilt The World homepage**.

Its purpose is to describe:

- section order,
- content hierarchy,
- visual emphasis,
- user flow,
- and layout behavior across desktop and mobile contexts.

This is not a design mockup and not a coded implementation.

It is a structural planning document for homepage composition.

---

## Homepage role

The homepage should function as a **gateway**.

It should not try to explain every layer of RTW in full detail.

Its job is to:

- establish identity,
- communicate the mission,
- create trust,
- show the layered nature of the system,
- and guide visitors toward deeper pages.

The homepage should feel clear, intentional, and serious.

---

## Structural principles

The homepage should follow these principles:

- strong first impression,
- short high-value sections,
- visible hierarchy,
- generous spacing,
- clear calls to action,
- minimal clutter,
- and progressive depth.

The page should move from:

1. identity,
2. to explanation,
3. to structure,
4. to mission,
5. to entry,
6. to continuity.

## Homepage section order

### 1. Hero
### 2. What RTW is
### 3. Why it exists
### 4. System layers
### 5. Mission extension
### 6. Entry paths
### 7. LAB and Archive
### 8. Signals
### 9. Closing call

---

## Section 1 — Hero

### Purpose
Create immediate recognition, atmosphere, and orientation.

### Content
- logo or logo + mark
- small eyebrow text
- main headline
- supporting paragraph
- primary CTA buttons

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- large centered or slightly left-aligned hero
- logo visible above or beside the main headline
- strong headline block
- CTA row below supporting text

Mobile:
- stacked layout
- logo first
- headline second
- support text third
- CTAs full-width or two-row layout

### Recommended visual weight
This should be the strongest visual section on the page.

### Notes
Do not overload the hero with too much theory.
The hero should create clarity and emotional direction, not density.

---

## Section 2 — What RTW is

### Purpose
Give a compact explanation in plain, stable language.

### Content
- short heading
- 1–2 concise paragraphs
- optional supporting sentence emphasizing continuity or structure

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- medium-width centered content block
- readable line length
- strong typographic hierarchy

Mobile:
- single-column
- short paragraphs
- generous spacing

### Notes
This section should reduce ambiguity quickly.
A first-time visitor should understand that RTW is a system, not just a slogan.

---

## Section 3 — Why it exists

### Purpose
Explain the human need behind RTW.

### Content
- heading
- short rationale
- emphasis on overlooked potential, self-directed growth, and real-world development

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- single-column narrative block
- optional side accent line or small visual divider

Mobile:
- compact text block
- no unnecessary visual complexity

### Notes
This section should feel human and grounded.
It explains the deeper reason without becoming abstract.

---

## Section 4 — System layers

### Purpose
Show that RTW is a layered environment.

### Content
- section heading
- short intro sentence
- 4 layer blocks:
  - Public Layer
  - Living LAB
  - Archive Layer
  - Proof Layer

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- 4 cards in grid or 2x2 structure
- each card with title + one short explanation

Mobile:
- stacked cards
- one per row

### Notes
This section is one of the most important structural blocks on the homepage.
It helps visitors understand depth without overload.

### Visual treatment
This section may use cards, subtle borders, icons, or line-based structure.

---

## Section 5 — Mission extension

### Purpose
Show the moral seriousness of RTW.

### Content
- heading
- short statement about child protection, vulnerable youth, dignity, access, and support
- optional CTA to dedicated mission page

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- highlighted block
- slightly different background or bordered panel
- serious, calm emphasis

Mobile:
- single-column emphasis block
- no decorative overload

### Notes
This section must feel sincere and grounded.
It should not look like virtue signaling or emotional decoration.

---

## Section 6 — Entry paths

### Purpose
Help different kinds of visitors find an appropriate way in.

### Content
- heading
- short intro
- 4 entry cards:
  - For Builders
  - For Thinkers
  - For Future Readers
  - For the Overlooked

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- 2x2 grid
- equal visual weight across cards

Mobile:
- stacked cards
- clear spacing between them

### Notes
This section translates the RTW philosophy into navigation logic.
It should feel welcoming without becoming generic.

---

## Section 7 — LAB and Archive

### Purpose
Show that RTW has both active experimentation and preserved memory.

### Content
- section heading
- two featured blocks:
  - The LAB
  - The Archive
- short bridge text below or between them

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- two-column split section
- LAB on one side, Archive on the other

Mobile:
- stacked blocks
- LAB first, Archive second

### Notes
This section is important because it shows that RTW is both living and durable.

### Visual treatment
Use strong card or panel treatment.
This section should feel like two complementary forces.

---

## Section 8 — Signals

### Purpose
Introduce dynamic inward paths.

### Content
- heading
- short explanation of what signals are
- optional future list of 3 recent signals

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- simple feed-like or card-based row
- can remain minimal in first version

Mobile:
- stacked small items or one short intro block only

### Notes
Signals do not need to be fully built in version one.
Even a placeholder structure is acceptable if the logic is clear.

---

## Section 9 — Closing call

### Purpose
End with direction, identity, and momentum.

### Content
- closing headline
- short final paragraph
- final CTA row

### Layout direction
Desktop:
- centered closing block
- strong spacing above and below
- clear final CTA emphasis

Mobile:
- stacked closing section
- full-width CTA buttons if needed

### Notes
This should feel calm, strong, and durable.
Not sales-like.

## Recommended mobile flow

Mobile should preserve the same section order.

### Priorities
- fast clarity
- strong headline
- readable spacing
- fewer horizontal layouts
- no visually dense cards
- clear CTA stacking

### Mobile notes
- collapse multi-column sections into vertical flow
- keep paragraph lengths short
- ensure hero remains strong without feeling oversized
- reduce visual effects further on small screens

---

## Visual spacing guidance

### Section rhythm
Use generous vertical spacing between sections.

### Content density
Prefer fewer words with better hierarchy.

### Card spacing
Cards should breathe.
No cramped grids.

### Visual silence
Allow dark space to carry mood and seriousness.

The homepage should not feel crowded.

---

## Interaction guidance

### Buttons
Use clear, stable labels.
No novelty wording.

### Hover states
Subtle.
No flashy motion.

### Animation
Minimal fade or rise effects only where needed.
No constant movement.

### Scroll behavior
Smooth and calm.
The structure should feel composed.

---

## Logo placement guidance

### Preferred first version
Use the logo prominently in the hero section.

### Optional second use
Small logo mark in the top navigation.

### Avoid
- repeating the full logo too many times
- placing the logo over busy backgrounds
- crowding the logo with competing decorative elements

The logo should function as an anchor, not wallpaper.
