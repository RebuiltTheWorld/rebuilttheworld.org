# RTW Design Direction
## Rebuilt The World
### Version: v0.1

---

## Purpose of this document

This document defines the first visual and interface direction for the **Rebuilt The World** website.

Its purpose is to describe:

- the visual atmosphere,
- the color direction,
- typography behavior,
- interface mood,
- logo relationship,
- and the design principles that should guide future web implementation.

This is not a coded style system.
It is a design direction document.

---

## Design intention

The RTW website should feel like a **mission-driven digital system**.

It should communicate:

- seriousness,
- clarity,
- human responsibility,
- structured depth,
- and long-term continuity.

The website should not feel decorative first.
It should feel intentional first.

---

## Core visual reading

The visual direction should combine five qualities:

### 1. Human strength
The site should carry a sense of agency, endurance, and moral seriousness.

### 2. Digital structure
The interface should suggest systems, connection, archives, and layered intelligence.

### 3. Calm intensity
The visual language should be strong without becoming loud.

### 4. Trustworthy depth
The design should imply that there is more beneath the surface.

### 5. Future readability
The site should feel built to last, not built for a passing trend.

---

## Overall mood

The intended mood is:

- dark-first,
- restrained,
- clean,
- spacious,
- focused,
- and quietly powerful.

The site should feel like a place where meaning is being built and preserved.

---

## What the site should not feel like

The website should avoid feeling like:

- a crypto hype page,
- a startup landing template,
- a gaming portal,
- a generic AI product site,
- or a neon cyberpunk experiment.

RTW should feel more like an institution-in-formation than a trend object.

---

## Color direction

### Primary background
Deep midnight blue or near-black navy.

### Secondary background
Dark blue-gray surfaces for panels and section separation.

### Primary accent
Cool cyan-blue.

### Secondary accent
Muted teal or green-blue.

### Text color
Soft white or cool off-white.

### Low-emphasis text
Blue-gray or muted cool gray.

### Border and divider color
Subtle dark steel-blue or blue-gray.

---

## Color behavior

Color should be used with discipline.

### Use accent colors for:
- buttons,
- active navigation,
- subtle highlights,
- key labels,
- and selected structural emphasis.

### Avoid:
- too many competing bright tones,
- rainbow accent systems,
- high-saturation gradients everywhere,
- or glowing effects on every component.

Accent should signal importance, not decoration.

---

## Surface direction

The site should use layered surfaces rather than flat sameness.

### Recommended surfaces
- dark page background,
- slightly lighter cards,
- subtle elevated panels,
- bordered content blocks,
- and occasional focus sections with stronger contrast.

### Surface principle
Each surface should help structure the page.
No surface should exist only for visual noise.

---

## Typography direction

Typography should support seriousness, legibility, and hierarchy.

### Headline style
- strong,
- geometric,
- wide,
- and stable.

### Body text style
- clean sans-serif,
- comfortable line height,
- highly readable at medium paragraph length.

### Tone of typography
The typography should feel:
- contemporary,
- disciplined,
- and not overly soft.

---

## Typographic hierarchy

### Level 1
Homepage hero headline and major section titles.

### Level 2
Subsection headings and layer labels.

### Level 3
Supporting text, card descriptions, and page-intro paragraphs.

### Level 4
Metadata, eyebrow text, and utility labels.

Hierarchy should be obvious without being theatrical.

---

## Layout philosophy

The layout should prioritize:

- strong vertical rhythm,
- generous spacing,
- clean alignment,
- limited clutter,
- and clear section boundaries.

The page should breathe.

Dense stacking of unrelated elements should be avoided.

---

## Spacing direction

Spacing should be part of the brand experience.

### The site should feel:
- open,
- deliberate,
- calm,
- and breathable.

### Avoid:
- cramped cards,
- crowded hero areas,
- tiny margins,
- or too many blocks pressed together.

Dark space is useful.
It reinforces seriousness and focus.

---

## Card and panel direction

Cards should be used as structural tools.

### Good card behavior
- clear title,
- short explanation,
- subtle border or panel distinction,
- generous padding,
- calm visual weight.

### Cards should not feel like:
- dashboard widgets,
- novelty tiles,
- or flashy app-store modules.

Cards belong to a system, not a toy interface.

---

## Button direction

Buttons should be:

- clean,
- readable,
- stable,
- and direct.

### Good button labels
- Explore the System
- Read the Manifesto
- Enter the LAB
- Open the Archive

### Visual direction
- one primary button style,
- one secondary button style,
- optional text-link style.

### Avoid
- overly rounded playful buttons,
- glowing neon buttons,
- or excessively animated hover states.

---

## Navigation direction

Navigation should be simple and calm.

### It should feel:
- structured,
- light,
- easy to scan,
- and not overfilled.

### First version recommendation
- logo
- About
- Manifesto
- LAB
- Archive
- Entry

Optional later:
- Signals
- Children & Support

Navigation should help orientation, not expose every layer immediately.

---

## Icon and graphic direction

Icons and supportive graphics should follow the logic of the logo.

### Recommended qualities
- simple geometry,
- line clarity,
- low visual noise,
- and consistency with the globe/network/system feeling.

### Avoid
- cartoon icons,
- glossy 3D packs,
- or generic corporate illustration sets.

Any graphics should feel native to RTW.

---

## Image direction

The site does not need many images in the first version.

### Preferred image logic
- logo-led branding,
- abstract system visuals,
- archive textures,
- dark diagrams,
- symbolic imagery used sparingly.

### Avoid
- stock-photo overload,
- smiling corporate portraits,
- random AI art without system meaning,
- or overly sentimental visuals.

Imagery should support structure and mood.

---

## Motion direction

Motion should be minimal and purposeful.

### Acceptable motion
- subtle fade-in,
- gentle rise on section reveal,
- soft hover transitions,
- and restrained navigation feedback.

### Avoid
- constant moving backgrounds,
- fast parallax,
- dramatic animation sequences,
- or distracting UI motion.

Movement should never compete with meaning.

---

## Logo relationship

The logo is a primary identity anchor.

The website should be designed to support the logo, not overpower it.

### Current primary use
- dark background placement,
- prominent hero usage,
- generous surrounding space.

### Avoid
- repeating the logo excessively,
- placing it over busy textures,
- recoloring it casually,
- or surrounding it with visual clutter.

The logo should feel grounded and central.

---

## Emotional target

A first-time visitor should feel:

- this is serious,
- this has depth,
- this is built with intention,
- this is human-centered,
- and this may be worth exploring further.

The design should generate trust before fascination.

---

## Accessibility direction

The first visual system should aim for:

- strong contrast,
- clear text readability,
- predictable navigation,
- low motion burden,
- and clean mobile behavior.

A dark interface should not reduce readability.
It should strengthen focus.

---

## Mobile direction

Mobile should preserve the same identity without becoming cramped.

### Mobile priorities
- readable text,
- strong section spacing,
- simple CTA stacks,
- reduced decorative effects,
- and clear vertical flow.

The mobile version should feel disciplined, not compressed.

---

## First implementation priorities

The first visual implementation should prioritize:

1. hero clarity
2. readable typography
3. strong section spacing
4. card consistency
5. button discipline
6. dark-surface harmony

Perfect polish is less important than structural coherence.
